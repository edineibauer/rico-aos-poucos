Strategy (MSTR): Bitcoin's Systemic Risk in 2026

Could the company that bet everything on Bitcoin be the next FTX? Complete analysis of debt structure, crisis scenarios and what happens if Bitcoin drops to $40,000.

Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) has become synonymous with institutional Bitcoin betting. Under Michael Saylor's leadership, the company transformed from a software company into a "leveraged Bitcoin vehicle." But is this strategy sustainable? And what happens if it goes wrong?

What is Strategy?

Founded in 1989, MicroStrategy was a business intelligence software company. In August 2020, Michael Saylor made a radical decision: use the company's cash to buy Bitcoin as a store of value.

Since then, the strategy has scaled aggressively. The company:

  • Renamed itself "Strategy" in February 2025
  • Accumulated 673,783 BTC (January 2026)
  • Spent over $50 billion on purchases
  • Became the largest corporate Bitcoin holder in the world

The current financial structure includes:

  • Convertible bonds: $8.2 billion in debt with average coupon of 0.42%
  • Preferred shares (STRK): $7.5 billion with 8-10% dividends
  • Stock sales (ATM): $21 billion already sold by May/2025

The Cash Flow Problem

Strategy needs to pay $779 million per year in interest and dividends. Its software business generates only $460 million in annual revenue. The math doesn't work without selling shares or issuing more debt.

In this scenario:

  1. Market cap would plummet: MSTR stock, which already fell 66% from its high, could lose another 50-70%
  2. Stock fundraising would be difficult: Nobody wants to buy shares of a company in crisis
  3. Debt refinancing would be expensive: Creditors would demand much higher rates
  4. Cash reserves would deplete: The $2.19 billion reserve would last ~21 months

The Critical Point

If Bitcoin falls below $16,500 and stays there until 2028, Strategy could become insolvent, according to VanEck analysis. Hedge fund internal models test $13,000 as theoretical insolvency line.

The Dangerous Feedback Loop

What makes Strategy especially risky is the feedback loop between stock price and Bitcoin price:

  1. Bitcoin price rises → MSTR stock rises (with 1.5-2x leverage)
  2. MSTR rising → Company sells shares to buy more Bitcoin
  3. Bitcoin buying → Upward pressure on price
  4. Cycle repeats...

The problem? The loop works both ways:

  1. Bitcoin price falls → MSTR stock plummets (with leverage)
  2. MSTR falling → Hard to sell shares, needs alternatives
  3. Pressure to sell Bitcoin → Amplifies the drop
  4. Downward spiral...

Strategy's Defenses

To be fair, Strategy has some protections:

  • Cash reserve: $2.19 billion (covers ~21 months of payments)
  • Uncollateralized debt: Most bonds don't have Bitcoin as collateral
  • Long maturities: Average of 5.1 years to maturity
  • 2022 experience: Survived the bear market without selling Bitcoin

Michael Saylor argues: "Bitcoin can go from $100,000 to $1,000. The debt won't be called early; there's no recourse."

The 2022 Test

In 2022, when Bitcoin fell 75% to ~$16,000, Strategy recorded an accounting loss of $1.28 billion. The mNAV (market value vs NAV) dropped to 0.7x. Even so, the company didn't sell any Bitcoin.

Impact on Bitcoin Price

Strategy holds 3.2% of all Bitcoin. If forced to sell, the market impact would be devastating:

  • Liquidating 673,783 BTC would flood the market
  • Widespread panic among institutional holders
  • Domino effect on other companies with BTC treasury
  • Potential additional 30-50% price drop

It's a "too big to fail" scenario for the Bitcoin ecosystem, but without any central bank to rescue it.

1. Is it an opportunity to buy MSTR?

We don't recommend it. The stock offers leveraged Bitcoin exposure, but with additional risks of dilution, debt and management. If you want Bitcoin, buy Bitcoin directly or via ETFs.

2. Is it structurally functional long-term?

Depends on Bitcoin. If Bitcoin keeps rising, Strategy will be a spectacular success. If it enters a prolonged bear market, the structure could collapse. There's no middle ground.

3. Is it safe against crises?

Definitely not. Strategy is the opposite of a defensive investment. It's a leveraged bet on a single volatile asset.

4. Could it be the next FTX/Terra Luna?

Unlikely, but possible. The probability of total collapse is estimated at 10-20% for 2026. If it happens, the impact would be greater than FTX.
