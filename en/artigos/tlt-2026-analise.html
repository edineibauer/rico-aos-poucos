The TLT ETF (iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond) tracks long-term US Treasury bonds and has been beaten down in recent years due to rising interest rates. However, we believe 2026-2027 presents an attractive opportunity for appreciation as rates stabilize and potentially decline.

What is TLT?

TLT is an ETF that holds US Treasury bonds with maturities of 20+ years. These are:

  • Backed by the US government: Considered the safest bonds in the world
  • Fixed income: Pay regular interest (currently ~4.5% yield)
  • Inverse to rates: When interest rates fall, bond prices rise (and vice versa)
  • Dollar exposure: Provides exposure to USD for international investors
üìä Key Fact: TLT has fallen ~50% from its 2020 highs due to the Fed raising rates from 0% to 5.5%. Most of this adjustment is now behind us.

1. Rates Near Peak

The Federal Reserve raised rates aggressively in 2022-2023 to combat inflation. With inflation now moderating, the Fed is likely done hiking. The next move is more likely to be cuts than further increases.

2026-2027?
Potential rate cuts

If recession or crisis hits, Fed will cut rates aggressively. TLT would rally strongly.

2. Recession Insurance

TLT is one of the best hedges against recession available. During economic downturns:

  • The Fed cuts rates to stimulate the economy
  • Investors flee to safety (US Treasuries)
  • Both factors push bond prices higher
  • TLT can rally 20-40% during major crises

üìà Historical Performance in Crises

2008 Financial Crisis: TLT gained 40%+ as Fed cut to zero

COVID-19 (March 2020): TLT rallied 20%+ in weeks

2023 Banking Crisis: TLT up 15%+ in one month

3. Attractive Yield PLUS Upside

At current prices, TLT offers an attractive risk-reward profile:

  • Base case: Earn 4.5% annual yield, backed by US government
  • Bull case: If rates fall to 3%, TLT could appreciate 20-30%
  • Bear case: If rates rise to 6%, TLT falls ~15% (limited downside from here)

This asymmetric payoff - limited downside, significant upside - is appealing in uncertain times.

Risks We're Monitoring

TLT is not without risks. Here's what could go wrong:

‚ö†Ô∏è Key Risk: Inflation Resurgence

If inflation returns above 5%, the Fed would need to raise rates further. This would push TLT lower. However, we believe this scenario is less likely given current economic indicators.

Other Risks

  • Opportunity cost: If stocks rally 30%, TLT's 4.5% will underperform
  • Dollar strength: For international investors, dollar appreciation helps, but dollar weakness hurts
  • Interest rate duration risk: Long-term bonds are more volatile than short-term

The Bull Case for TLT

Here's our optimistic scenario for 2026-2027:

  1. Q1-Q2 2026: Economic data softens, recession concerns grow
  2. Q3 2026: Fed signals rate cuts are coming
  3. Q4 2026: First rate cuts of 0.25-0.50%, TLT rallies 10-15%
  4. 2027: If recession hits, Fed cuts aggressively to 3%, TLT rallies another 15-20%

In this scenario, TLT could return 30-40% total over 18-24 months (yield + capital gains).

How to Invest in TLT

There are several ways to gain TLT exposure:

  • Direct ETF: Buy TLT on US exchanges (requires international brokerage)
  • BDR: Buy BIGT39 on B3 (Brazilian exchange) - tracks TLT
  • Individual bonds: Buy 20-30 year US Treasuries directly

Conclusion

We are bullish on TLT for 2026-2027 for these reasons:

  1. Down ~50% from peak - most rate hike pain is behind us
