Brazilian Stocks in 2026: Time for Caution?

Ibovespa rose 34% in 2025, but risks and opportunities for the coming year

Sentiment for Brazilian Stocks in 2026

The Ibovespa had an excellent 2025, rising 34%. This strong performance raises an important question: is there still room for more gains in 2026, or is it time for caution?

2025 in Review: What Drove the Ibovespa

Several factors contributed to the 34% rise in the Ibovespa in 2025:

  • Dollar appreciation: Real depreciation benefits exporters
  • Commodity prices: Iron ore and oil remained strong
  • Interest rate expectations: Market anticipated Selic cuts
  • Valuation recovery: Brazilian stocks were very cheap

Why Caution in 2026?

‚ö†Ô∏è Multiple Risk Factors

2026 brings several challenges for Brazilian stocks: election year, growing deficit, high interest rates, and fiscal uncertainty. The easy gains have already been captured.

After a 34% rally, part of the upside potential has already been realized. Now we face:

  • 2026 election year: Uncertainty and increased volatility
  • High Selic (15%): Makes fixed income more attractive
  • Growing deficit: Fiscal situation continues deteriorating
  • Valuations normalized: Stocks are no longer as cheap
  • Global slowdown risk: US and China with challenges

Election Year: Friend or Foe?

Election years in Brazil historically bring:

üìâ Negative Points

  • Increased volatility
  • Political uncertainty
  • Fiscal populism
  • Postponed reforms
  • Capital flight risk

üìà Positive Points

  • Increased spending (temporary boost)
  • Opportunities in volatility
  • Possible change of direction
  • Oversold stocks

Promising Sectors

Despite caution, some sectors may perform well:

üí° Opportunities in Specific Sectors

  • Exporters: Benefit from weak real and commodity demand
  • Banks: High spreads and credit expansion
  • Retail: If Selic really falls, consumption recovers
  • Energy: Growing demand and infrastructure investments

Comparison with Fixed Income

With Selic at 15%, fixed income offers extremely attractive risk-free returns. This makes the cost of investing in stocks higher:

  • Government bonds: 15% per year without risk
  • Treasury IPCA+: 7% real return guaranteed
  • Stocks: Need to beat these returns to be worthwhile

üéØ High Opportunity Cost

For stocks to be attractive at Selic 15%, they need to offer significantly higher potential returns. This limits the investment case for Brazilian equities.

Recommended Strategy

Our position is neutral for Brazilian stocks in 2026:

  1. Small allocation (5%): Participate without overexposure
  2. Sector selection: Focus on exporters and banks
  3. Wait for opportunities: Buy dips during election volatility
  4. Long-term view: Don't trade short-term noise
  5. Dollar hedge: Exporters provide natural dollar exposure

Recommended Allocation

We maintain the recommendation of 5% exposure to Brazilian stocks. This conservative allocation:

  • Provides exposure to potential upside
  • Limits risk from election year volatility
  • Keeps capital available for better opportunities
  • Reflects the high opportunity cost of Selic at 15%

Conclusion

Brazilian stocks had an excellent 2025, but 2026 brings considerable challenges:

  • Ibovespa already rose 34%, easy gains captured
  • 2026 is an election year with inherent uncertainty
  • Selic at 15% makes fixed income very attractive
  • Fiscal situation continues to deteriorate

Our recommendation is small allocation (5%) with sector focus. Prioritize exporters and banks. Use election volatility to enter positions gradually. Most importantly, maintain a long-term perspective and don't get caught up in short-term noise.
