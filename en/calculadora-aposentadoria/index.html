Fundamental Concepts

The Rules of Retirement

Understand the principles that guide financial planning

4%

Withdrawal Rule

You can withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually without depleting the principal. With $1M, that's $40,000/year or $3,333/month.

25x

Required Net Worth

Multiply your annual expenses by 25. If you spend $5,000/month, you need $1,500,000 for independence.

72

Rule of Doubling

Divide 72 by the interest rate to know how many years it takes to double your money. At 10% p.a., it doubles in ~7 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions & Answers

How much do I need to retire?
The general rule is to have 25x your annual expenses. If you spend $5,000/month ($60,000/year), you need $1,500,000 invested. With this amount earning 4% per year above inflation, you can live off the income indefinitely.
What is the 4% rule?
It's a guideline based on studies (Trinity Study) suggesting you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually without depleting the principal over 30+ years. With $1 million, you could withdraw $40,000/year, adjusted for inflation.
How does compound interest work?
Compound interest is interest on interest. If you invest $1,000 at 10% per year, in the first year you earn $100. In the second, you earn 10% on $1,100 ($110), and so on. The effect is exponential in the long run.
What is FIRE?
FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. It's a movement that seeks to achieve financial freedom as early as possible through a high savings rate and investments.
Should I consider inflation?
Yes! The calculator works with real returns (above inflation). If inflation is 5% and your investment earns 10%, the real return is ~5%. Always use real values for planning.
