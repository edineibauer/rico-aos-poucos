Frequently Asked Questions

Questions and Answers

How to build a diversified portfolio?
A diversified portfolio should contain assets from different classes: fixed income (CDs, Treasury bonds), variable income (stocks, REITs), hedges (dollar, gold) and emergency reserve. The ideal proportion depends on your risk profile, age and financial goals.
What's the ideal allocation for beginners?
For beginners, it's recommended to start with higher weight in fixed income (60-70%) and gradually increase exposure to variable income as you gain experience. The important thing is to start and adjust over time.
Should I invest in dollars?
Having part of your portfolio in dollars (10-25%) serves as protection against currency devaluation and local crises. This can be done via currency funds, ETFs or directly in dollar-denominated assets abroad.
What is rebalancing?
Rebalancing is the process of adjusting your portfolio periodically to maintain the original allocation. If stocks have risen a lot and now represent 40% (target was 30%), you sell part and reallocate to sectors below target.
Is my portfolio saved?
Yes! Your portfolio is automatically saved in the browser (localStorage). When you return to the site, your configuration will be there. No need to create an account or log in.
