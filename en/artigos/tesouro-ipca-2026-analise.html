Treasury IPCA+ in 2026: High Rates, Real Risk

Inflation-indexed bonds offering IPCA+7% look attractive, but fiscal risk cannot be ignored

Sentiment for Treasury IPCA+ in 2026

Treasury IPCA+ bonds are currently offering rates around IPCA + 7% per year, which at first glance seems very attractive. However, the Brazilian fiscal scenario brings a real risk that cannot be ignored: what if the government can't pay?

What is Treasury IPCA+?

Treasury IPCA+ is a government bond that pays a fixed rate plus inflation. For example, if you buy IPCA+ 7%, you will receive 7% above inflation annually.

üí∞ Real Return Guaranteed

The big advantage is real return protection. If inflation is 5% per year, you receive 5% + 7% = 12% nominal. Your money always maintains purchasing power plus the agreed real gain.

Why IPCA+7% Seems Attractive

A real rate of 7% per year is historically high. This means that:

  • Protection against inflation: Your money always maintains purchasing power
  • Predictability: You know exactly how much you'll gain in real terms
  • Government guarantee: In theory, the government backs the bonds
  • Low volatility: Holding until maturity eliminates mark-to-market risk

The Big Problem: Can the Government Pay?

üö® Brazilian Fiscal Situation

Brazil is in a delicate situation. The Debt/GDP ratio is at 79% and rising. The primary deficit is 9% of GDP. The government spends more than it collects, and the debt snowball keeps growing.

The question that needs to be asked is: will the government be able to honor these bonds in the long term? There are some worrying scenarios:

  • Forced haircut: Government unilaterally reduces bond values
  • Higher inflation: Prints money to pay debt, destroying purchasing power
  • Repudiation: In an extreme scenario, declares inability to pay
  • Forced conversion: Exchanges IPCA+ bonds for less favorable ones

2026: Election Year Increases Uncertainty

2026 is an election year in Brazil, which historically brings:

  • Increased spending: Governments tend to spend more to win votes
  • Fiscal irresponsibility: Unpopular measures are postponed
  • Market volatility: Uncertainty about who will win
  • Risk premium increase: Investors demand more yield to hold Brazilian assets

Recommended Strategy

Despite the attractive rates, our position is neutral for Treasury IPCA+ in 2026:

  1. Moderate allocation: Don't put all eggs in one basket
  2. Diversify maturities: Mix short and long-term bonds
  3. Watch the scenario: If fiscal situation worsens, reduce exposure
  4. Consider alternatives: REITs, real estate, dollar also protect against inflation

Treasury IPCA+ should be part of the portfolio, but not the only strategy. The 7% real rate is tempting, but the fiscal risk is real and growing.

Recommended Allocation

We maintain the recommendation of 10% exposure to Treasury IPCA+. This allocation:

  • Captures the attractive real rate
  • Doesn't overexpose to government risk
  • Leaves room for other inflation protection strategies

Conclusion

Treasury IPCA+ with rates of 7% is tempting, but you need to keep your eyes open:

  • The real rate is historically high and attractive
  • But the Brazilian fiscal scenario is deteriorating
  • 2026 is an election year, increasing uncertainty
  • Government solvency risk cannot be ignored

Our recommendation is to have Treasury IPCA+, but with moderation. Diversify with other assets that also protect against inflation but don't depend exclusively on the government.
