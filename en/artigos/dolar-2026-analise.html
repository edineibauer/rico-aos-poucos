Dollar drop in 2025 - largest since 2016

The dollar closed 2025 with its largest annual drop since 2016, falling 11.17% against the Brazilian real. This may seem contradictory to our bullish view, but it's precisely this drop that creates the opportunity. The market anticipated a scenario that may not materialize as expected.

Dollar as a Safe Haven Asset

One of the main arguments for our bullish view is the dollar's role as a global safe haven asset. Historically, during times of crisis or market stress, the dollar appreciates:

  • 2008 Crisis: Dollar appreciated even with the crisis originating in the US
  • COVID-19 (2020): Flight to quality led investors to the dollar
  • Emerging market crises: Any turbulence leads to capital outflows

Selic Reduction and Capital Outflows

With Selic at 15%, Brazil attracts foreign capital through carry trade - investors borrow money in countries with low interest rates and invest here. When Selic falls to 12% or less, this interest rate differential decreases, and part of this capital leaves.

This means demand for dollars - investors selling reais to buy dollars and send them back to their countries.

Fiscal Risk

The Brazilian fiscal scenario remains concerning. High public spending, difficulty in meeting fiscal targets, and political uncertainties create a risky environment for the real.

Election years in Brazil historically bring exchange rate volatility. Uncertainty about the continuity of economic policies may pressure the real.

Historical Perspective

Looking at the long-term history, the dollar has always appreciated against the real. In 1994, 1 dollar was worth 1 real. Today, it's worth more than 6 reais. This reflects:

  • Inflation differential between countries
  • Higher productivity of the American economy
  • Status as a global reserve currency
  • Periodic crises in Brazil

The 11% drop in 2025 is a correction within a secular uptrend. We see this as an entry point, not as a trend reversal.

Recommended Allocation

We maintain our recommendation of 15% dollar exposure in the portfolio. This can be done through:

  • Account at an international brokerage (Interactive Brokers, Avenue, etc.)
  • American fixed income ETFs
  • American stocks directly
  • BDRs of American ETFs on B3

Conclusion

We are bullish on the dollar for 2026-2027 for the following reasons:

  1. 11% drop in 2025 created attractive entry point
  2. Geopolitical scenario favors US (Venezuelan oil, strong economy)
  3. Dollar is a safe haven asset in crises - and we see risks on the horizon
  4. Brazilian Selic reduction will decrease carry trade
  5. Fiscal and electoral risk in Brazil
  6. Historical trend of dollar appreciation vs real

Currency exposure is not speculation - it's geographic diversification and protection against the inherent risks of having all your wealth in a single currency.
